Welcome to Lone Star!

We're happy we could have you here to enjoy making stories with us as we go.
As any other community, we have our own unique ruleset, and it is required
that you take heed of the guidelines as you play and enjoy the community.

Server Rules

Category A: Common Sense and Courtesy

A.1) Act in good faith. The spirit of a rule supersedes its wording.

A.2) Do not spam, troll, grief, or otherwise intentionally ruin the game
        for others.

A.3) Do not harass other players. If someone expresses OOC discomfort,
        stop immediately and rethink what you are doing. Sexual harassment will
        not be tolerated. Deadchat is not a protected zone from moderation.

A.4) Do not participate in sparking drama or arguments. Do your best to
        keep the peace.

A.5) Do not use slurs under any IC or OOC circumstance. Bigotry is
        strictly prohibited and will be met with an instant QC ban with an 
        unlikelihood for an appeal.

A.6) Do not share in-character events from an ongoing round through
        OOC means. This is IC in OOC and spoils the round for others.

A.7) Cooperate with staff when ticketed. Do not lie or verbally abuse staff
        in a ticket. If a staff member tells you to do something in an
        administrative capacity, you must comply.

A.8) Respect the rulings of staff members. If you want to contest a ruling,
        file a ticket on the server Discord.

Category B: Immersion and Atmosphere

B.1) Do not break immersion for other players. Maintain the atmosphere of
        Fallout’s setting at all times.

  • Lone Star mostly seeks to highlight the more serious elements of Fallout.
    “Wild wasteland” and other silly — but immersion-breaking — elements are
    generally not permitted. “Fallout is wacky” is not an excuse for disrupting
    the atmosphere. Exceptions apply for unanimous admin approved "Wild
    Wasteland" rounds - to be properly implemented in the future. Despite this,
    being funny or unusual for the sake of others' enjoyment is acceptable if it
    is executed well.

B.2) Do not reference elements of modern culture in-character. Fallout
        canon diverges from real life around the 1950’s. Memes have no place in
        our setting.

  • Bruh”, “femboy”, “soyboy”, “beta cuck”, “based”, “sus”, “sneed”, “cope and
    seethe”, “LARP”, etc. are all examples of terms that must not be used
    in-character. This list is not exhaustive; use your own judgement.

B.3) Name your characters appropriately. Your character name must be
        suitable for the setting and atmosphere of Fallout. It is up to staff
        discretion whether or not a name is appropriate for the setting.

  • Your name may not be a reference to famous individuals, real or fictional.
  • Gag names or overly explicit/slur names are not permitted.
  • Members of the Brotherhood of Steel and the NCR must have both a first
    and last name; no nicknames or titles.
  • Legionaries must have Romanized names.

B.4) Do not explicitly reference OOC mechanics such as respawning,
        AHELPing, admin-healing, round timers, etc. When in doubt, use LOOC, or
        obscure your language such that it fits within the setting and does not hurt
        immersion.

  • Please do not refer to staff as “the gods” in-character. Use language
    appropriate for your role (i.e. “High Command” for NCR, “Mars” for Legion,
    “the Elders” for BoS, etc.)

B.5) Do not play high responsibility roles without a deep understanding
        of the faction you are playing, as well as the mechanics involved
        with leading from the front. A Centurion or NCR captain not
        understanding their own command structure, a Follower administrator not
        knowing how to do surgery or chemistry, are examples that are deeply
        immersion-breaking.

B.6) No End-of Round Grief (EORG) outside of the train. Maintain
        immersion until the round ends. The train exists to allow end of round
        TDM to occur in that area to let player steam off.

Category C: Heavy Roleplay Standards

C.1) Lone Star is a roleplay-heavy server. Players must be relatively fluent in
        English and capable of coherent communication.

  • Players are expected to use punctuation and proper grammar/spelling.
    Egregious spelling errors and an overly simple way of speaking may also
    mandate an age-check by an admin. Failure to meet this standard may
    result in administrative action.

C.2) Continuity and canon persists between rounds. Previous decisions and 
        actions will carry over to the next round over. Events may be decanonized
        if all players involved agree, or if staff deem it necessary.

  • Player character deaths are to be retconned as serious injuries. Avoid
    referencing the fact that you have died in-character; approach it as though
    you were merely unconscious, comatose, or otherwise incapacitated.
  • Players may opt to make their own character deaths permanent; it is their
    discretion. However, if a character voluntarily commits suicide, it will
    be considered a permanent character kill.
  • You may not escalate to lethal on player characters over events from prior
    rounds.
  • If a result of an in-character investigation by an admin-level faction leader
    (NCR Colonel, Legate, Elder, etc), any execution as a result of this will be
    considered a permanent character kill.     A majority of admin-level
    faction leaders will not escalate to this unless it is a last resort or very
    egrigious situation.

C.3) You must roleplay a consistent character and respond to in-character
        events like a real person would. Characters that are deemed unrealistic or
        unsuitable to Fallout’s setting may be subject to administrative action.

  • Natural hair colors only. Hair dye exists, but keep it tasteful.
  • Reasonable height; anyone taller than 7 feet or shorter than 4 feet should
    have significant deformities and must be roleplayed accordingly.
  • Reasonable appearance; you may not be a half-deathclaw mutant or a
    furred anthropomorphic creature. If you would like special privileges to
    play a non-standard character, ask server staff.

C.4) You are NOT the protagonist. Your character must have limitations to
        their knowledge and skills, such as a Legionary generally being unlikely to
        know much about chemistry or medical science.

C.5) Metaknowledge is prohibited; your character may only possess
        knowledge that they would reasonably attain, either through their
        background or through in-character events. There is a separation between
        what you as a player know, and what your character knows.

  • Do not communicate with other players out-of-character to gain
    in-character advantages — this is known as metacommunications.
  • Always keep in mind that your character (in most cases) was born in the
    23rd century, with little to no knowledge of pre-war America. The only
    exceptions to this are pre-war ghouls, and to a lesser degree
    Followers of the Apocalypse and the Brotherhood of Steel.
  • Characters who are related (i.e. siblings, parent/child, etc.) may not
    share info off-screen.     This is still considered metagaming.

C.6) Do not job-hop between roles or factions without in-character progression
        and strong reasoning. Inform at least one admin over discord if you plan
        to do this for good reason, so they can let the team know.

Category D: Mechanics

D.1) In all cases, roleplay supersedes mechanics. Do not prioritize
        mechanics for roleplay; treat them as a supplement to RP.

  • If an otherwise reasonable action cannot be represented mechanically,
    players are allowed and encouraged to represent that action through
    their roleplay. Other players are expected to respect this.
  • That said, no godmodding/powerplaying. This is taking control of
    another player's character during roleplay, and is generally not permissible.

D.2) Do not powergame. Powergaming is defined as abusing game mechanics
        in a way that is damaging to roleplay, especially in a way that is unrealistic
        for a character to be capable of.

  • Exploiting dungeons to complete them in an unintended way through abuse
    of mechanics is considered powergaming.
  • Some other examples of powergaming, generally speaking:
    • A BoS Paladin mixing complex medicines
    • A Follower Doctor solo-wiping a faction
    • A Legionary with no medical training performing surgery.

D.3) Do not utilize exploits, do not share exploits with other players. Report
        them privately to staff or our development team.

D.4) Do not mechanically engage with players who are SSD, AFK, or
        otherwise unresponsive but alive.

  • Do not abuse this protection. Intentionally disconnecting to avoid
    consequences for one’s actions will result in administrative action.
  • If you must disconnect for the entire round, please despawn yourself
    at a Matrix point, especially if you are a high responsibility role.

D.5) You may not, in any fashion, render someone unrevivable, without
        first adminhelping in order to request permission to "round remove"
        a character indefinitely from the round. Put simply, if you kill someone
        in a standard fashion, even in a raid, loot them then leave them. It is,
        however, permissible to kill someone and then revive them in order to take
        them prisoner, enslave them, or more officially execute them through
        roleplay means later, as long as you are attempting to ensure they are able
        to roleplay for the rest of the round.

  • Caveat: If the other player in LOOC is fine with your intended manner of
    executing their character and does not report the incident, we do not
    consider this problematic.
  • The list that is by no means complete as to what is considered rendering
    someone unrevivable: burial inside a raid-protected faction base,
    decapitation, gibbing, brain destruction, bonfiring, excessive offensive
    chemical overdoses, etc.
  • Bounty hunters should adminhelp to request permission from either the
    player they killed or an admin for guidance on claiming their bounties.
  • Repeatedly bad faith characters that consistently conduct themselves poorly
    in a single round-basis can have these protections revoked by an admin.

D.6) Off-duty roles must not act as regular on-duty faction members.
        Off-duty personnel must defer authority to those on-duty. Do not pull rank
        when off-duty.

  • If a faction is being attacked, off-duties MAY NOT defend their base. They
    are required to matrix. Only engage in faction combat if it is self-defense.
    If a superior officer orders you to defend the base when off-duty, please
    AHELP so we can remind them that this is OOCly disallowed.
  • Off-duty BoS may not leave the bunker.

D.7) Faction members must wear faction armor in combat. They may not
        discard their faction armor in favor of unaffiliated or generic armor. Painting
        generic armor in faction colors does not quality it as faction armor.

Category E: Escalation and Combat

E.1) In nearly all circumstances, combat MUST have ample escalation and
        in-character justification. Do not escalate on another player without
        strong IC reasoning.

  • NEVER INITIATE, OR PARTICIPATE IN COMBAT WITHOUT
    ACTIVATING YOUR COMBAT INDICATOR. IF INITIATING, WAIT
    A FEW SECONDS FOR THE OTHER PLAYER'S REPLY, UNLESS
    ALREADY BEING SHOT AT OR ATTACKED.     The default button to toggle
    this is C.
  • Follow the steps indicated in the Escalation Flowchart, found on the wiki's
    Rules Page.
  • If you provide conditions to someone while escalating (i.e. "If you pull a
    gun, you're dead!") and they actively take an action that violates those
    conditions, you are free to attack them.

E.2) Do not use bolas without escalating. Never throw a bola without
        activating combat mode. Un-escalated bolas will be punished as harshly
        as any other form of poorly-escalated combat.

E.3) Do not handcuff others without escalating, either via verbal warning
        or an emote that clearly presents your intention to handcuff them.

  • If a person is unconscious or involved in an ongoing fight, this is not
    necessary.

E.4) If a player flees from active escalation, they may be attacked
        immediately.

  • This explicitly refers to players attempting to run away from a roleplay
    confrontation when the instigator activates combat intent. We will not
    consider this "no-esc" or "gank".
  • If a player attempts to shoot, handcuff, strip, disarm or stun another
    without escalation/combat indicator active, they may be attacked
    immediately. Report the individual via adminhelp or through the complaints
    tickets on Discord if no staffmember is present.
  • Dragging away bodies, healing combatants, or otherwise aiding members of
    an active fight is accepting escalation. Medics have no Geneva Convention
    rights, this is the post-apocalypse.

E.5) If more than two minutes pass between combat, players are
        required to re-escalate.

E.6) Faction members may join fights involving other faction members if
        and only if both participants are wearing their faction uniform, with their
        tags (if applicable) clearly visible.

  • NCR and Legion should remain in uniform unless given explicit approval for
    a covert infiltration (via raid approval), in which case they may wear each
    others' gear explicitly for that purpose.
  • Non-faction members must make their intentions and allegiance clear
    before joining combat, faction v. faction or otherwise.

E.7) Base raids, faction or player, ALWAYS must require explicit
        permission from online staff. If you wish to raid a base you must
        AHELP for permission.

  • Base raids require clear in-character justification. Staff may deny raids if
    they are unjustified or if the balance of power is grossly disproportionate.
    As the round progresses later into the game, larger-scale raids will be
    permitted with more leniency, but not with an unfair balance of power.
  • Retaliatory raids will be denied if the initial raid incurred major casualties
    on the attacker. We don't do double jeopardy here.
  • Refer to the Guide to Raiding for a more comprehensive description of
    server rules and standards regarding base raids.

E.8) Do not escalate on or steal from other players without provocation
        until at least 30 minutes into the round. If someone ignores this rule
        and tries to rob or kill you anyway, you may defend yourself, as long as you
        report it after so we can discourage this behavior.

Category F: Mature Content

F.1) This is an 18+ community. Underage users will be swiftly removed,
        without exception. Refusing an age check if you are suspected to be
        underage will result in a permanent ban from the server until you pass the
        age check through an appeal.

  • The staff team reserves the right to perform non-invasive age-checks on
    any member of the community.
  • All characters that appear in-game must be over 18. Underage
    characters are not permitted under any circumstance.

F.2) Mature content is permitted. This includes erotic roleplay. Explicit
        consent is required before engaging in sexual content, either in-character,
        in OOC notes, or in LOOC. Consent may be withdrawn at any point.

  • It is expected that players treat ERP as an extension of regular roleplay,
    rather than a primary focus. Hypersexual characters may be moderated.
  • Do not harass other players for engaging in ERP. If you cannot tolerate
    mature content, leave.

F.3) Non-consensual Erotic Roleplay is forbidden. No non-consensual ERP
        may take place between any player characters. Given the nature of the
        setting, sexual assault and similar content still exists within the universe
        and it may still be used as part of a character's backstory, but it should
        never take place on our server or be canonized between player characters.

F.4) Extreme content is not permitted. This includes (but is not limited to)
        any depiction of sexual gore, scatophilia, pedophilia, vorarephilia, or
        bestiality. Keep it off our server.

  • Players may use LOOC to opt out of any objectionable content,
    within reason. Please be respectful of others and their sensitivities.

F.5) Do not include oversexualized descriptions in flavor text. Any explicit
        reference art must be clearly marked as NSFW.
